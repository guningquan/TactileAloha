TactileAloha: Learning to Manipulate with Tactile Sensing

Ningquan Gu
Mitsuhiro Hayashibe*
arXiv (coming Soon) Hardware Code ML Code

Abstract

Tactile texture is vital for robotic manipulation but is sometimes difficult to observe. To address this issue, we propose TactileAloha, a tactile sensing module mounted on the Aloha system to provide texture information of touched objects, enhancing manipulation capabilities. During the training process, tactile information is encoded by ResNet and combined with encoded camera images before being input into an Action Chunking with Transformers-based policy. The policy then outputs a sequence of predicted actions, which is used to compute the loss against ground truth actions. We define a novel loss function, assigning higher weights to more recent actions in the prediction sequence while exponentially decaying the weights of actions that are further away in the sequence. To enhance performance, we co-train the policy with the previous Aloha dataset, despite its lack of tactile information and the presence of redundant data. During deployment, we integrate Temporal Proximity Ensembling, which queries the policy at each timestep and aggregates the corresponding predicted actions within a preset temporal span. To enhance precision, more recent predictions are assigned higher importance, ensuring the policy remains focused on recent actions. This method improves action accuracy while preserving smoothness. Experimentally, we introduce two tasks: zip-tie insertion and Velcro fastening, both of which are highly sensitive to the grasping orientation of objects. Tactile sensing helps perceive the object's orientation, enhancing manipulation accuracy. For the two tasks, we collect 80 and 50 demonstrations for training. The results demonstrate that our approach, leveraging gripper tactile information, is capable of handling texture-related tasks, which cannot be addressed by camera vision-based methods. Moreover, our method achieves an average improvement of approximately 11.0% compared to SOTA, demonstrating its performance.
